Brick by Brick

A LEGO stop motion I shot in Grade 10 for Mr. Shalaby's graphical communication class. One photo per nudge of the bricks.

Grade 10 · graphical communication Mr. Adam Shalaby LEGO on a desk · no CGI

How it got made

In Grade 10 I took graphical communication with Mr. Adam Shalaby. One of the assignments was a short stop motion. I built a small LEGO scene on my desk, named it Brick by Brick, and filmed it by moving the pieces a little, taking a picture, moving them again. That was the whole trick.

Stop motion sounds simple until you are halfway through and realise how easy it is to bump the camera or knock a brick out of place. I spent a lot of afternoons on it. I liked the part where the stack of photos finally plays back and the bricks look like they move on their own. That feeling is why I still have the project.

I do not remember which editing program I used. I imported the photos in order, set the timing, and exported. The gif here is that export: the same timing as the original, 12 frames per second, a little under 32 seconds start to finish.
