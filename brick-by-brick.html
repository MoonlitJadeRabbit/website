Brick by Brick

A hand-animated LEGO stop-motion short from Grade 10 — posed, shot, and sequenced one frame at a time.

Grade 10 · graphical communication Mr. Adam Shalaby Hundreds of LEGO frames · no CGI

What this was

Brick by Brick is a hand-animated stop-motion short built from hundreds of LEGO photographs. Each frame was posed, shot, and sequenced into a short film — frame by frame, with no CGI.

I made it in Grade 10 for a graphical communication course that introduced video editing workflows. The piece was a class assignment under Mr. Adam Shalaby. It was one of the more enjoyable projects that year — mostly because watching still frames turn into motion never gets old.

I no longer remember which editing program I used back then; the workflow was standard stop-motion: capture a frame, nudge the bricks, repeat, then assemble the sequence and export. The version here adds a quiet ambient bed for the web player only (credited above).
